# Executive Summary

## Overview

This project presents the results of a cybersecurity risk assessment conducted for GoldLeaf Accounting LLC, a simulated small business operating in a remote home-office environment.

The objective of this assessment was to identify key assets, evaluate potential cybersecurity threats, and recommend improvements to reduce overall cyber risk.

## Key Findings

The assessment identified several cybersecurity risks that could impact business operations:

* Phishing attacks targeting employee credentials
* Ransomware infections affecting employee laptops
* Weak password practices
* Insecure home WiFi configurations
* Malware infections from malicious downloads

These risks could lead to unauthorized access to sensitive financial data, disruption of accounting operations, and potential data loss.

## Risk Overview

The highest priority risks identified were:

* Credential compromise from phishing attacks
* Ransomware infection of employee laptops
* Weak authentication practices

These risks have both **high likelihood and high potential impact** on business operations.

## Recommended Improvements

To reduce cybersecurity risk exposure, the following actions are recommended:

1. Enable multi-factor authentication for all cloud accounts
2. Deploy endpoint protection software on employee laptops
3. Implement employee cybersecurity awareness training
4. Enforce strong password policies
5. Establish regular data backup procedures

## Conclusion

By implementing the recommended security controls, GoldLeaf Accounting LLC can significantly strengthen its cybersecurity posture and reduce the likelihood of successful cyber attacks.

This assessment demonstrates how structured cybersecurity risk analysis can help organizations prioritize security improvements and protect critical business systems.
