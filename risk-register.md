# Cybersecurity Risk Register

## Overview

This document lists identified cybersecurity risks for GoldLeaf Accounting LLC based on the previously identified assets and threats.

Each risk is evaluated using two factors:

* Likelihood (how likely the event is to occur)
* Impact (how severe the damage could be)

Risk levels help prioritize which security issues should be addressed first.

---

## Risk Register

### Risk 1 — Phishing Attack Leading to Credential Theft

Description:
Employees may receive phishing emails that trick them into entering their login credentials on a malicious website.

Affected Assets:

* Email system
* Employee credentials
* Cloud services

Likelihood: High
Impact: High

Risk Level: Critical

Recommended Mitigation:

* Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)
* Conduct employee phishing awareness training
* Deploy email filtering tools

---

### Risk 2 — Ransomware Infection on Employee Laptop

Description:
An employee could open a malicious attachment that installs ransomware and encrypts company files.

Affected Assets:

* Employee laptops
* Cloud storage
* Accounting software

Likelihood: Medium
Impact: High

Risk Level: High

Recommended Mitigation:

* Maintain secure backups
* Install endpoint protection software
* Apply regular system patching

---

### Risk 3 — Weak Passwords or Credential Reuse

Description:
Employees may reuse passwords across multiple services, increasing the risk of account compromise.

Affected Assets:

* Employee credentials
* Email system
* Cloud services

Likelihood: Medium
Impact: High

Risk Level: High

Recommended Mitigation:

* Enforce strong password policies
* Implement password managers
* Require multi-factor authentication

---

### Risk 4 — Insecure Home WiFi Networks

Description:
Employees working from home may use poorly configured WiFi networks, which could allow attackers to intercept traffic.

Affected Assets:

* Home WiFi networks
* Employee laptops

Likelihood: Medium
Impact: Medium

Risk Level: Medium

Recommended Mitigation:

* Require WPA2 or WPA3 WiFi security
* Encourage router firmware updates
* Provide employee security guidance for home networks

---

### Risk 5 — Malware Infection

Description:
Employees could accidentally download malicious software from websites or email attachments.

Affected Assets:

* Employee laptops
* Company data

Likelihood: Medium
Impact: Medium

Risk Level: Medium

Recommended Mitigation:

* Deploy endpoint protection tools
* Restrict software installation permissions
* Conduct security awareness training
